# The Governance Architecture

## Introduction

### Goal
Design the security governance structure that MedDefense needs to execute and sustain a security program.

### Context
A strategy without governance is a document that sits on a shelf. Governance defines who is responsible, who makes decisions, who is accountable and how the program is sustained beyond the initial implementation.

James Chen raises this concern: "Right now, security decisions are made by whoever shouts loudest. Sarah thinks she owns endpoint security because IT manages the endpoints. I think I own it because it is a security function. Dr. Patel in Cardiology thinks he can do whatever he wants with his data because he is a physician. We need clear roles."

## Answer

# Part 1 – RACI Matrix

| Activity | CEO | Deputy CISO (James) | IT Director (Sarah) | Dept Heads | Security Analyst (You) |
|----------|-----|----------------------|---------------------|------------|------------------------|
| Security budget approval | **A** | **C** | C | **I** | C |
| Vulnerability remediation | I | **A** | **R** | **C** | **C** |
| Incident response execution | I | **A** | **R** | **C** | **R** |
| Security policy approval | **A** | **R** | C | **C** | C |
| Risk acceptance decisions | **A** | **R** | C | **C** | I |
| Security awareness training | I | **A** | C | **R** | C |
| Vendor risk assessment | I | **A** | C | **C** | **R** |
| Audit coordination | I | **A** | C | **C** | **R** |

### Legend
- **R – Responsible:** Performs the activity.
- **A – Accountable:** Final owner and decision-maker.
- **C – Consulted:** Provides input before decisions are made.
- **I – Informed:** Kept informed of outcomes.

# Part 2 – Role Definitions

| Role | Assigned To | Explanation |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Data Owner** | Department Heads (Clinical, HR, Finance, Radiology, etc.) | Business owners responsible for the data generated by their department. They approve access, define classification, and determine business requirements. |
| **Data Controller** | MedDefense Health Systems (represented by the CEO/Executive Management) | Determines why and how personal data is processed and is legally accountable for compliance with healthcare privacy regulations. |
| **Data Processor** | Third-party providers (Microsoft 365, cloud backup providers, external laboratories, SaaS vendors) | Process MedDefense data on behalf of the organization according to contractual agreements. |
| **Data Custodian (Steward)** | IT Director (Sarah) and the IT Operations Team | Implement and maintain technical controls including backups, patching, encryption, monitoring, and access permissions. |

# Part 3 – The CISO Question

The absence of a dedicated CISO leaves MedDefense without an executive responsible for cybersecurity governance, enterprise risk management, and Board reporting. James, as Deputy CISO, can coordinate operational security but cannot fully assume strategic leadership while managing daily security activities. Given the fixed **$120,000 annual security budget**, hiring a full-time CISO (typically exceeding the entire available budget) is not financially realistic. A **vCISO** provides executive governance, compliance oversight, risk management, and Board reporting at a much lower annual cost, allowing MedDefense to invest the remaining budget in higher-impact initiatives such as MFA, vulnerability management, SIEM/MDR, and network segmentation. As the security program matures and funding increases, transitioning to a full-time CISO should be reconsidered.
