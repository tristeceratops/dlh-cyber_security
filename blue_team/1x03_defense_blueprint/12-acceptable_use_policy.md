# The Policy Draft

## Introduction

### Goal
Draft an Acceptable Use Policy for MedDefense that is grounded in identified risks, enforceable and realistic for a hospital environment.

### Context
Frameworks give structure. Controls give protection. Policies give authority. Without a written, approved policy, a security team cannot enforce anything. When the billing clerk plugs in a personal USB drive, the response is either "please do not do that" (informal, unenforceable) or "you are violating Section 3.2 of the AUP, which was signed during onboarding and approved by the CEO" (formal, enforceable, documented).

This task is unique because it requires you to think about security from the perspective of the people who have to live with it. A policy that forbids everything is ignored by everyone. A policy that is unreasonably strict for clinical staff will be worked around. A good policy is one that people can actually follow.

ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (AUP)

Policy Owner: Deputy CISO
Approved By: Executive Management
Effective Date: [Insert Date]
Review Cycle: Annual

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Acceptable Use Policy defines the appropriate use of MedDefense Health Systems information systems, networks, applications, and devices. It applies to all employees, contractors, physicians, vendors, students, and third parties with access to MedDefense resources.

This policy covers:
- Electronic Health Record (EHR) systems
- PACS and medical imaging systems
- Billing and financial systems
- Hospital workstations and servers
- Network resources, VPN access, email, cloud services, and connected medical devices

The purpose is to protect patient safety, Restricted PHI, financial information, and MedDefense operations from unauthorized access, misuse, data loss, and cyber threats.

2. ACCEPTABLE USE OF SYSTEMS

Users may use MedDefense systems only for authorized clinical, administrative, research, and operational activities.

Employees are permitted to:
- Access patient records required for their job responsibilities.
- Use approved applications and systems provided by MedDefense.
- Use internet and email resources for legitimate business activities.
- Store and process MedDefense information only on approved systems.
- Report suspected security issues, phishing attempts, or system problems.

Users must:
- Follow least-privilege access rules.
- Lock workstations when unattended.
- Verify recipients before sending sensitive information.
- Use only assigned accounts and credentials.

3. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

The following activities are forbidden because they increase identified MedDefense risks:

Unauthorized access:
- Accessing patient records without a legitimate job requirement.
- Sharing accounts or using another employee’s credentials.
- Attempting to bypass access controls.

Data exposure:
- Sending Restricted PHI, billing data, or credentials to personal email accounts or cloud storage.
- Copying patient information to unauthorized devices.
- Using personal NAS, Gmail, or Google Drive services for MedDefense data.

Network and system compromise:
- Installing unauthorized software or devices on the MedDefense network.
- Connecting unmanaged devices such as unauthorized Raspberry Pi systems.
- Disabling security controls such as antivirus, monitoring, or encryption.
- Attempting security testing without authorization.

Medical device risks:
- Changing settings on medical devices without approval.
- Connecting medical equipment to unauthorized networks.

4. PERSONAL DEVICES AND REMOVABLE MEDIA

Personal devices create security risks identified in MedDefense assessments, including shadow IT storage and unrestricted USB usage.

USB drives:
- Personal USB drives may not be used to store or transfer MedDefense data.
- Only approved encrypted removable media may be used.
- USB activity may be restricted or monitored.

Personal phones:
- Personal phones may access approved services only through authorized methods.
- Patient data must not be stored locally on personal phones.
- Photos of patient information or clinical screens are prohibited.

Personal laptops:
- Personal laptops may not connect directly to MedDefense internal networks.
- Remote access requires approved security controls, including MFA.

5. PASSWORD AND AUTHENTICATION REQUIREMENTS

All users must:
- Use unique passwords for MedDefense accounts.
- Never share passwords or authentication codes.
- Use MFA when accessing VPN, administrative systems, and externally exposed applications.
- Report suspected credential compromise immediately.

Administrative users must use dedicated administrator accounts and must not perform daily activities using privileged accounts.

6. DATA HANDLING REQUIREMENTS

MedDefense classifies information as:

Restricted:
- Patient PHI
- EHR records
- Medical imaging data
- Credentials
- Sensitive financial information

Restricted data must:
- Only be accessed by authorized personnel.
- Be stored only on approved systems.
- Be encrypted when required.
- Not be copied to personal devices or unauthorized cloud services.

Internal:
- Operational documents and internal communications.
- Must only be shared with authorized employees.

Public:
- Information approved for external release.

Employees must verify data classification before sharing, storing, or transmitting information.

7. MONITORING AND ENFORCEMENT

MedDefense monitors systems to protect patient safety, security, and compliance requirements.

Monitoring may include:
- Network activity.
- VPN connections.
- Login activity.
- Security alerts.
- Access to sensitive systems.
- Data transfers and removable media activity.

Security monitoring does not remove employee responsibility for protecting information.

Violations may result in:
- Access removal or restriction.
- Mandatory security training.
- Disciplinary action up to termination.
- Legal action when required.

Suspected security incidents must be reported immediately to the IT or Security team.
