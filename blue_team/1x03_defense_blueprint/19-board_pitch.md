# The Board Pitch

## Introduction

### Goal
Deliver the entire strategy in 300 words or fewer.

### Context
Same drill, fourth time. Board meeting Monday. James Chen gives you the brief: "This time, you are not just reporting findings. You are asking for money. The Board needs to approve the $120,000 spend. You have 300 words to convince them."

The pitch must answer exactly four questions: Where are we today ? What is at stake ? What do we recommend ? What do we gain ?

## Answer

MEDDEFENSE BOARD SECURITY INVESTMENT PITCH

Current State:
After 4 weeks of assessment, MedDefense has strong basic security controls but remains exposed to major risks from ransomware, weak identity protection, limited monitoring, and flat network access. The verdict is simple: MedDefense can prevent some attacks, but it cannot reliably detect, contain, or recover from a major cyber incident.

The Risk:
If no action is taken, a ransomware attack could disable critical hospital systems, expose patient data, and interrupt clinical operations. Our analysis estimates the ransomware risk at approximately $300,000 in expected annual loss (ALE), including recovery costs, downtime, and regulatory impact. A single successful attack could create far higher losses than the annual estimate.

The Plan:
We recommend a $120,000 security improvement program focused on the three highest-impact controls: multi-factor authentication ($8,000), network segmentation ($20,000), and improved monitoring through SIEM/MDR ($25,000), combined with resilient backups and endpoint protection. These controls reduce the likelihood of stolen credentials, limit attacker movement inside the hospital, improve early detection, and increase recovery capability. The program is expected to reduce millions of dollars in potential annual cyber risk exposure by addressing the attack paths most likely to affect patient care and operations.

The Return:
For approximately every $1 invested, MedDefense avoids an estimated $30+ in expected cyber loss based on the reduction of identified risks. This investment is not only a security expense; it is a patient safety, operational continuity, and financial protection decision.
