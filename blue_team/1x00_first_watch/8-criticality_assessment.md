# 8. The Criticality Assessment

## Introduction

### Goal
Evaluate the criticality of each asset category using CIA-based analysis, calibrated to the specific operational context of a healthcare organization.

### Context
"Not all assets are equal," says James Chen. "If the cafeteria's digital menu board goes down, nobody dies. If the EHR goes down, physicians are prescribing blind. I need you to tell me which assets matter most and why, in terms the Board will understand."

## Answer

| Asset Category | Confidentiality | Integrity | Availability | Overall Criticality | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| EHR System | Critical | Critical | Critical | Critical | The EHR stack ([A-001](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-002](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-032](./7-asset_registry.md)) contains active patient charts, orders, medications, results, and portal-linked clinical data. A confidentiality breach triggers HIPAA notification, litigation exposure, and loss of patient trust; an integrity failure can alter allergies, orders, or lab values; and an outage leaves physicians, nurses, and pharmacists working blind. |
| PACS / Imaging | Critical | Critical | Critical | Critical | Imaging assets ([A-003](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-021](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-022](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-030](./7-asset_registry.md)) support radiology diagnosis and procedure planning. Image confidentiality is sensitive PHI, but integrity is the more dangerous issue because a mislabeled or altered study can drive the wrong treatment decision. If PACS or the MRI workflow is unavailable, radiology slows down or stops and inpatient care is delayed. |
| Core Identity & Directory | High | Critical | Critical | Critical | Active Directory and supporting identity services ([A-005](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-006](./7-asset_registry.md)) control authentication across EHR, file services, clinical workstations, and many networked systems. Compromise of identity integrity enables staff impersonation and privilege escalation; loss of availability prevents logon across the hospital and quickly becomes a clinical outage, not just an IT outage. |
| Network Core, Perimeter & Wireless Infrastructure | Medium | Critical | Critical | Critical | The firewall, core switching, and site edge devices ([A-013](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-014](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-015](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-016](./7-asset_registry.md)) determine whether MedDefense can segment, filter, and route traffic at all. These devices do not usually contain much patient data, so confidentiality is lower, but tampering with ACLs, VPNs, or routing can expose internal systems or bypass controls. If they fail, the hospital loses inter-site connectivity, internet access, and potentially all clinically critical network paths. |
| Backup & Recovery Infrastructure | High | Critical | Critical | Critical | Backup orchestration and storage ([A-009](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-010](./7-asset_registry.md)) are the main recovery path after ransomware or destructive failure. The data is highly sensitive because backups duplicate almost every regulated record in the environment; integrity is critical because a corrupted backup is a false safety net; and availability is critical because the business cannot restore EHR, file services, or imaging within acceptable downtime windows without it. |
| Medical IoT Clinical Devices | High | Critical | Critical | Critical | Clinical devices ([A-023](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-024](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-025](./7-asset_registry.md)) support bedside monitoring, infusion, and nurse escalation. Confidentiality matters because they expose patient telemetry and care context, but the real risk is integrity: a manipulated pump setting, false monitor reading, or failed nurse-call path can directly affect patient safety. Availability is critical because these systems sit on the front line of ICU, ER, and ward operations. |
| Clinical Endpoints | High | High | High | High | Workstations and thin clients used by clinicians ([A-017](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-020](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-019](./7-asset_registry.md)) are not the source of truth, but they are the primary access path to it. A compromise leaks PHI and credentials; tampering can distort orders or documentation; and an outage forces slower manual workflows, which is tolerable for short periods but disruptive across a large clinical footprint. |
| Administrative Endpoints & Collaboration | High | High | Medium | High | HQ workstations, laptops, and Microsoft 365 ([A-018](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-019](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-033](./7-asset_registry.md)) handle HR, finance, legal, procurement, and day-to-day collaboration. Confidentiality and integrity are high because they hold payroll data, contracts, email, and internal documents; availability is important but usually recoverable through alternate communications and paper-backed procedures if needed. |
| Billing Infrastructure | High | High | High | High | Billing and claims processing ([A-004](./7-asset_registry.md)) contain patient and payer data and are directly tied to revenue flow. If records are exposed, MedDefense faces regulatory and contractual consequences; if data is altered, claims are denied or fraudulently redirected; and if the server is unavailable, the organization may still care for patients but will quickly accumulate receivables, backlogs, and cash-flow pressure. |
| Physical Security & Life Safety Systems | High | Critical | Critical | Critical | Badge access and life-safety systems ([A-026](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-025](./7-asset_registry.md)) protect doors, controlled areas, and alerting paths for staff and patients. Integrity is critical because a false badge decision or manipulated access rule can let an unauthorized person into a server room, pharmacy, or clinical area; availability is critical because a failure can block legitimate emergency access or interrupt urgent staff notification. |

Notes on category placement: the two undocumented hosts from the network scan ([A-027](./7-asset_registry.md), [A-028](./7-asset_registry.md)) are treated as unmanaged server risk inside the network/core exposure picture rather than as a separate business category, because their main risk is unknown ownership and uncontrolled services.

### Top 5 Most Critical Assets

1. **[A-001 ehr-srv-01](./7-asset_registry.md)** - This is the front door to the clinical record and application tier. If it fails, clinicians lose access to orders, results, medication history, and patient context; if it is compromised, attackers can tamper with active workflows at the point of care.

2. **[A-002 ehr-db-01](./7-asset_registry.md)** - The database is the system of record for the EHR. A breach here is worse than an application outage because it can expose the most sensitive PHI at scale and corrupt the source data that every downstream clinical decision depends on.

3. **[A-005 ad-dc-01](./7-asset_registry.md)** - Primary identity services are a hospital-wide dependency, not a back-office convenience. If this domain controller is unavailable or compromised, users cannot authenticate cleanly into EHR, file shares, portal services, and many clinical systems, creating immediate operational paralysis.

4. **[A-021 MRI-CTRL-WS (WS-RAD-01)](./7-asset_registry.md)** - This legacy control workstation is the choke point for MRI operations and already carries an explicit end-of-life risk. Because it bridges the scanner to PACS transfer, compromise or failure can halt radiology throughput and create a patient-safety issue if imaging is delayed or mishandled.

5. **[A-013 FortiGate-100F](./7-asset_registry.md)** - The perimeter firewall is one of the few controls standing between MedDefense and unrestricted exposure. If its policy, VPN, or segmentation function is lost, the organization can no longer rely on network boundaries to contain a breach, protect sensitive services, or separate clinical from non-clinical traffic.
